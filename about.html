about me

In 2016, I graduated Cum Laude with a Bachelor's Degree in Neuroscience at Wellesley College, MA, USA. While there, I got the chance to dissect sheep brains, and enervated the large motor neurons of crayfish. I also worked as a research assistant in the Mechanisms of Affect and Dysregulation Lab ("MADLab" - pun intended), where I used EEG to identify a frontal asymmetry profile associated with irritability.

Currently, I'm finishing my second Bachelor's in Integrated Computer Science at the University of British Columbia, which has now given me the ability to build and create applications from ground zero. I am also a member of UBC's Visual Cognition Lab, where I help build new visualization experiments that test our ability to assess correlation.

My multidisciplinary background makes me naturally inquisitive of the interaction between humans behavior and technology. How can our understanding of human cognition drive design? What new technological mediums can arise from this knowledge? These are only some of the questions that I hope to get a chance to explore in the future.
