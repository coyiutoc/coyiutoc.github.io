Modeling the Effects of Neurogenesis on Interference and Pattern Separation



WHEN:
January - April 2016

WHAT:
Final Project for Computational
Neuroscience Course

WHERE:
Wellesley College, MA, USA

TOOLS:
MATLAB
Pattern separation is the computational process that minimizes interference of similar memories through the creation of distinct, non-overlapping neural codes. Studies suggest that neurogenesis may influence pattern separation in the dentate gyrus, a structure that provides input to the hippocampus. This project serves to replicate the findings of Finnegan and Becker (2015) by using a simplification of the deep learning algorithm, the Restricted Boltzmann machine.
