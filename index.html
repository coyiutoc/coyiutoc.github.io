academic project : pixelplayer

may 2016

For a data structures class, designed a Java GUI in a team of 3. Pixelplayer allows users to draw on the 9x8 matrix by selecting 1 of 4 colours, where each colour corresponds to a specific note that is played as a chord once user is finished.

Was primarily in charge of sound production and back-end, using hashtables and queues for color-sound associations.

Java Swing

academic project : ear training tool

april - may 2015

With a partner, designed an ear training application using MATLAB. Depending on what "level" the user chooses, the GUI will randomly produce single notes, intervals or chords, and tests the user whether they are able to identify each correctly.

neuroscience research & projects

independent study: resting EEG asymmetries and levels of irritability

spring 2016

Awarded Wellesley College Student Library Award for Independent Study.

Study aims to investigate a potential EEG asymmetry profile for irritability. Self-reported measures of irritability and baseline EEG levels were assessed in undergraduates. Participants performed an attention-based task under conditions of frustration and non-frustration. This task served as the frustration manipulation to elicit irritability experimentally.

spring 2016

Final paper for Computational Neuroscience course.

Used a simplification of the deep learning algorithm, the Restricted Boltzmann machine, to model effects of neurogenesis in the dentate gyrus and hippocampus.

about me

I graduated Cum Laude with a Bachelor's Degree in Neuroscience at Wellesley College, MA, in 2016. While at Wellesley, I worked as a research assistant in the Mechanisms of Affect and Dysregulation Lab, where we studied irritability using behavioral and EEG measures. There, I learned how to read and analyze brain signals, which was pretty darn cool. I also know how to apply a 36-electrode EEG cap onto someone's head, which makes them look like a mad scientist.

I was also a music minor at Wellesley, where I would participate in 4-hour chamber music marathons and DJ my way through my electronic music class. Undoubtedly a musicophile, I can play the piano, cello and flute, and was winner of the 2016 Brandeis-Wellesley Concerto Competition.

Currently, I'm pursuing my second Bachelor's degree in Computer Science at the University of British Columbia. While here, I've played around with data structures and have come to realize the beauty of high level programming languages after squinting through polymorphism in Assembly. Due to my Neuroscience roots, I'm pretty interested in HCI and UX/UI, mostly because of my fascination with human behavior, and how technology is capable of shaping the ways in which we learn and think.

In my spare time, I'm usually playing JRPGs on portable consoles, dabbling around with web design, or hunting down the best dim sum restaurants in Vancouver!
